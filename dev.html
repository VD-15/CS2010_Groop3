07 / 10 / 2019

Hey everyone, watch out! We’re taking over with our brand-new game, Takeover! Bad puns aside, we are T3, Team 3 of CS2010 (a very creative team name I know). This marks our first of many blog posts in which we will update you where we are in the development of our game.

Creating the game concept for Takeover was no easy task, we just had so many ideas for our game which made it difficult to decide for what kind of game we wanted to create. However, after much thought about this matter, T3 presents... Takeover!

Gamers! Do you think you have what it takes? Partake in this galactic war between factions and play as a warlord to reign supreme over your opponent! Takeover is a suspenseful Sci-Fi Real-time Player Vs Player strategy game, play as your chosen commander and dominate all your foes. Build different units to make up your army and defeat the opposing enemy by claiming all that is theirs and finally defeating the opponent’s own commander, it’s a 1 vs 1 battle to see who is the best! See how well you do control and managing your army and expanding your empire but let me tell you now this is no easy task!

So there you have it, this is the game concept which we came up with for now and of course we will only continue to make our game even better to ensure a thrilling game experience for our gamers. But for now, we’ll keep you updated by posting updates on what’s happening with the game and how the development is going through these blog posts, therefore stay tuned!

After the submission of the hack we will focus on the other complex implementations of Takeover such as 3D rendering capabilities, creating unit models etc.

04 / 11 / 2019

Today’s meeting, we had a lot to discuss as we are facing a very big issue on the game development side and the website side of things. On Saturday we got a message from the team member developing the custom engine informing us on a serious problem which can take a very long time to fix and the dead line is closing in on us and we have yet not got any of the game developed yet and we need to start it. The issue in question is that the developer is getting driver crashes and also the compatibility of the engine is a huge risk and the developer believes has unable to diagnose the problem. He gave us 2 options on how we can move forward as a team to create the game

Option 1: Transition over to C/C++ in which fortunately, we have a back-up custom game engine.

Option 2: Switch over to Unity.

And today we discussed what path to take and we spoke about the pros and cons of each path, unity may be the easiest one to develop a game on, but it is hard to maintain with git as it creates a lot of temporary files. We chose to take the C/C++ path as the developer has a backup custom engine that works and also, he has a very good understanding of that language and he can write us a document on how C/C++ works and the basic syntax on how to code in it.

We hope the path we chose Is the right one.

On the other hand the website team is having an issue with the resizing of the boxes in the commander section of the website as it is not compatible with different screen sizes which they need to fix as we want to reach as many people as possible and make it viewable on all screen sizes and they are now discussing on what to do next in order to solve this problem.

11 / 11 / 2019

This is the day we have the meeting with the mentors for this project and they will give us feedback and advice on our submission luckily, we have submitted everything on time and completed.

We have been told we have a huge flaw with our structure with in the team and our development process which we lack of, we have also been told we don’t have the sense of completion as somethings have been not fully been written out and coming out of these meetings we have realised what we need to do to get the higher marks and be better as a team. Straight after the meeting with the mentors we decided to have a group meeting and address the issues and improve our group structure.

What we have done is added milestones on our GitHub and assigned each other to those milestones so we always know what we have to do and know what the others are doing. We have divided people into sub groups within our group to work on a common goal which are the game development team and the website team and each team has set up their own tasks and everyone can see it and assign that task to themselves so everyone in the group knows that, that specific task is being worked on.



We have also addressed the issue with the lack of a development process, and we have set one up now and implemented it on our GitHub which is the Kanban process.

The website has been addressed with the sense of completion by removing all filler text and actually adding meaningful text to the website giving it a complete feel.



(The Game Engine part had filler text which i does not anymore)

Overall, we got some great feedback from the mentors and we have worked on the feedback and now we have much more structure within our team, and we can show the structure to anyway with simply opening up our GitHub page.
